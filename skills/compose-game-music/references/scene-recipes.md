# Scene Recipes

Use these as starting points, not rigid rules.

## Selection Rules

- If the game is visually busy, choose a simpler motif and leave more high-frequency space for SFX.
- If the scene is mechanically repetitive, add phrase-level change every 8-16 bars.
- If the player must react quickly, avoid long attacks and full-spectrum pads.
- If the game has a signature mechanic, make one musical layer react to that mechanic.

## Cyberpunk Puzzle Action

- Tempo: 124-138 BPM
- Meter: 4/4
- Scale: D minor, D Dorian, or E Phrygian for threat
- Motif: short 3-5 note rising cell answered by a falling minor third
- Layers: sub pulse, metallic hats, filtered chord stab, thin lead, danger alarm
- Adaptation: add lead after first ability; add high alarm for hazards; add octave doubling near final zone

## Exploration

- Tempo: 85-112 BPM
- Scale: Dorian, major pentatonic, suspended modes
- Layers: soft pulse, sparse bass, long pad, occasional motif
- Avoid dense drums unless the area is hostile.
- Adaptation: add detail for landmarks, secrets, or weather/time changes.

## Hot Desert Field

- Tempo: 96-126 BPM depending on travel speed
- Scale: Phrygian, harmonic minor color, Dorian, minor pentatonic, or Mixolydian with flat-second color for heat shimmer
- Motif: short curved phrase with a leap or slide-like turn; repeat with ornament variation
- Layers: warm drone/air plane, plucked ostinato, hand-drum or frame-drum-like pulse, low pedal, reed-like lead, metallic/shaker texture
- Adaptation: add brighter shimmer for noon heat, thin percussion for ruins/night, add low pulse near danger
- Avoid: making it only "mysterious"; a field cue still needs walking motion.

## Hellfire / Infernal Field

- Tempo: 78-112 BPM, often with half-time weight or 12/8-style mass rather than a light travel groove
- Scale: Phrygian dominant, harmonic minor, chromatic minor-second/tritone cells, or modal pedal with unstable upper voices
- Motif: short descending judgment cell or rising heroic cell crushed by a lower answer; repeat with registral expansion, not decorative sparkle
- Surface: low sub/organ-like pressure, distorted bass or low-brass-like calls, choir-like or string-like cluster swells, tremolo heat field, slow ash/noise tail, sparse metal/stone impacts
- Motion: use bass gravity, harmonic pressure, and large swells as the walking force; percussion should feel like geology or ritual weight, not a pop backbeat
- Form: A establishes heat pressure; A' widens register; B adds oppressive choir/organ-like mass; final phrase opens a brief heroic line against the hell surface
- Adaptation: add high scream/heat layer near lava, stronger low impacts near danger, thinner surface in ruins/dialogue
- Avoid: treating it as a normal desert cue, using cheerful plucks/shakers, relying on snare/backbeat, or making it only ambient without forward motion.

## Snowfield / Frozen Country

- Tempo: 60-96 BPM
- Scale: Dorian, Aeolian, Lydian, suspended major, pentatonic with open fifths
- Motif: wide-spaced notes, descending breath, or small fragile cell answered by a warmer phrase
- Layers: cold wind/noise plane, soft pad, bell/chime-like points, slow bass, distant choir-like or string-like plane
- Adaptation: add high shimmer for open snow, low drone for blizzard, warmer harmony near settlements
- Avoid: constant high sparkle; it becomes UI-like and fatiguing.

## Combat

- Tempo: 130-165 BPM
- Scale: minor, Phrygian, harmonic minor color
- Layers: choose from low pulse, culturally appropriate percussion, bass ostinato, short chord hits, lead fragments, or tension texture; do not assume a kick/snare grid.
- Adaptation: increase hats and register with enemy count or boss phase.
- Avoid: lead melody that competes with attack/hit readability.

## Boss Fight

- Tempo: 140-178 BPM, or half-time at 70-89 BPM for huge bosses
- Scale: minor, Phrygian dominant, harmonic minor, chromatic accents
- Motif: short boss identity cell, often rhythm-first; answer with player theme fragment if relevant
- Layers: low ostinato or pedal, non-default impact language, mid stabs, high tension texture, phase lead, danger riser, silence/held threat
- Adaptation: phase 1 establishes pulse; phase 2 adds counter-rhythm/lead; final phase doubles bass or raises harmonic tension
- Stingers: phase change, stagger, near-death, victory release
- Avoid: using the same snare/backbeat vocabulary across unrelated boss cues.

## Chase / Runner

- Tempo: 150-190 BPM or 120-150 BPM with 16th motion
- Scale: minor, Dorian, Phrygian, pentatonic for readable arcade tone
- Motif: 2-4 note hook that can repeat without fatigue
- Layers: pulse, kick/snare or clap, bass ostinato, arps, danger ticks, occasional fill
- Adaptation: map speed/distance/threat to percussion density, octave, filter brightness, and fills
- Avoid: starting at maximum density; leave escalation headroom.

## Racing

- Tempo: 132-174 BPM
- Scale: minor pentatonic, Dorian, Mixolydian, or bright minor
- Layers: driving kick, sidechain-like pulse, bass riff, rhythmic chords, lead hook
- Adaptation: add high percussion/lead for boost, thin during crash, stinger for lap/final stretch
- Avoid: overlong chord changes that weaken forward motion.

## Rhythm Action

- Tempo: match chart needs, usually 100-180 BPM
- Scale: simple tonal center with strong downbeat identity
- Layers: clear drums, bass, chord hits, call-response motif
- Adaptation: sections should mark gameplay pattern changes; SFX must be rhythmically compatible
- Avoid: syncopation that fights player input timing unless intended.

## Horror / Tension

- Tempo: 60-90 BPM or pulse-only
- Scale: chromatic clusters, minor seconds, tritone color
- Layers: drone, bowed/noise texture, sparse low hits
- Avoid constant jumpscares; make silence part of the loop.
- Adaptation: threat proximity increases pulse rate, register, dissonance, and breath/noise texture.

## Horror Chase

- Tempo: 130-170 BPM or irregular pulse
- Scale: chromatic, Phrygian, tritone/minor-second cells
- Layers: heartbeat/sub pulse, distorted hits, scraping noise, siren-like motif, breath/riser
- Adaptation: chase start uses stinger then locks to pulse; escape removes high danger layer first
- Avoid: dense melodic content; urgency should come from rhythm and texture.

## Puzzle / Logic

- Tempo: 72-118 BPM
- Scale: pentatonic, Dorian, Lydian, suspended harmony
- Motif: small neutral cell that tolerates long repetition
- Layers: soft pulse, pluck, pad, occasional counterline
- Adaptation: add clarity/reward color when the player makes progress; thin when idle
- Avoid: emotional overstatement or heavy drums.

## Factory / Automation

- Tempo: 110-140 BPM
- Scale: minor pentatonic, Dorian, Mixolydian, whole-tone for quirky machines
- Motif: mechanical rhythmic cell, repeated with small phase shifts
- Layers: clockwork percussion, low pulse, metallic plucks, filtered stabs
- Adaptation: each production tier adds a synced mechanical layer
- Avoid: chaotic unsynced loops unless representing breakdown.

## Stealth

- Tempo: 70-115 BPM or pulse-only
- Scale: minor, Dorian, chromatic neighbor tones
- Layers: low pulse, muted percussion, sparse bass, tension pad, alert motif
- Adaptation: suspicion adds tick/pulse; alert adds drums/high ostinato; safe state removes transients
- Avoid: constant dramatic melody while the player is trying to listen.

## Cozy Village / Farm

- Tempo: 70-105 BPM
- Scale: major pentatonic, Mixolydian, Dorian, simple major
- Motif: warm 4-8 note phrase with gentle repetition
- Layers: light pluck, soft bass, brush/shaker, pad, occasional countermelody
- Adaptation: time of day changes instrumentation; rain/night removes bright percussion
- Avoid: saccharine constant high bells.

## JRPG Town

- Tempo: 78-112 BPM
- Scale: major, Mixolydian, Dorian, pentatonic
- Motif: memorable singable phrase; use simple cadences
- Layers: plucked/string-like harmony, soft bass, light percussion, woodwind-like lead
- Adaptation: shop/inn/menu can use simplified motif variation
- Avoid: battle-level drum density.

## JRPG Dungeon

- Tempo: 80-124 BPM
- Scale: minor, Dorian, Phrygian, suspended modal harmony
- Motif: short mysterious cell, often lower register
- Layers: pulse, pad, sparse percussion, occasional lead echo
- Adaptation: deeper floors add bass weight, dissonance, or rhythmic urgency
- Avoid: too much harmonic resolution; preserve exploration tension.

## Visual Novel / Emotional

- Tempo: 60-95 BPM
- Scale: major, relative minor, Dorian, suspended chords
- Motif: sparse and memorable, room for dialogue
- Layers: piano/pluck-like motif, pad/string-like support, soft bass
- Adaptation: use motif reharmonization for hope, grief, confession, uncertainty
- Avoid: busy rhythms that distract from reading.

## Shop

- Tempo: 85-125 BPM
- Scale: major, Mixolydian, pentatonic
- Motif: small loopable identity; friendly but not exhausting
- Layers: light bass, pluck, soft percussion, quirky accent
- Adaptation: rare item or purchase success can reuse motif as stinger
- Avoid: over-bright high loops that fatigue repeated visits.

## Victory / Reward

- Tempo: same as area or slightly faster
- Scale: lift motif into major third, sixth, or brighter mode
- Keep it short: 1-4 bars for small rewards, 8-16 bars for major clear.

## Menu / Title

- Tempo: 80-120 BPM
- Goal: identity, not intensity
- Use the main motif in a simpler arrangement.
- Adaptation: title can be fuller; pause/settings should be thinner and less distracting.
